Chateaugay Archival Center

The Chateaugay Archival Center houses the physical holdings of the Chateaugay Historical Society. The Archival Center is the repository for the Society’s information relating to the history and development of the geographical area encompassing the Town of Chateaugay, New York, including Chateaugay village.

Collections
The Archival Center is the result of the Chateaugay Historical Society’s mission of gathering and collecting informational materials through gifts, purchases, permanent donations or loan. Among the items collected in the Archival Center are books, pamphlets, monographs, manuscripts, newspapers, maps, drawings, photographs, prints, various business ledgers, audio and video tapes, films, CDs, microfilm, etc. All collections are stored in a secure, stable and proper archival environment, and are available to those who wish to make use of them. 

Public Resources
Our Center includes shelves of materials for the public to use in their research. These rich resources include:

  • Business Information
  • Church and Cemetery Records
  • Census Records for Chateaugay
  • Early Chateaugay Phone Directories
  • Family Genealogies
  • Local Area Histories
  • Local News Clippings (1946 - Present)
  • Obituaries
  • Sanborn Maps
  • School Information
  • AND More!

Research
The center has two computers and printers available for anyone doing historical or genealogical research at the center. Each computer has bookmarked links to various databases and websites which relate to the area’s history and appropriate genealogical resources.
